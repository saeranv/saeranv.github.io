Notes by Saeran Vasanthakumar --- 20/09/23 - Dynamic Modeling Thoughts
For the past year I’ve been intermittently toying around with designing a building energy model that attempts to predict real-time thermodynamic state in response to changes in geometry and material. Typical energy modeling tools require a user to rigorously abstract a building into a simplified set of thermodynamic boundaries, properties, and processes, which are then integrated over a given time period to represent a static representation of state. Of course, with machine and statistical learning it’s possible to cut through these abstractions and model energy flows in real-time. This isn’t a novel idea, and there are numerous implementations of ML-based energy predictions floating around, but I think there are various gaps in the problem domain that could use further exploration. I am particularly interested in the idea of experimenting with composable ML architectures that can dynamically model the spatial impact of geometry and matter. Earlier this year, I made a proof-of-concept that predicted thermal loads in simple shoe-box models with just a linear regression. I was able to get some decent results, but quickly found that it suffered when trying to account for the (nonlinear) impact of radiation. That was in March, and since then I’ve been busy moving cities, and switching jobs to properly revisit the problem - although I spend an inordinate amount of my day thinking about the issue. Luckily, it looks like I might have an excuse to revisit the problem soon. The purpose of this note was to revisit the problem domain and gather my constantly-evolving thoughts on it. The next step will be to scope out a simple, hackable problem that can serve as the next iteration of this idea of a dynamic model.

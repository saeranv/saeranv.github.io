Spatial energy transfer $$ \begin{flalign} Q_x &= k A \frac{dT}{dx} &\text{ [W]} &\\ \end{flalign} $$ Temporal energy transfer $$ \begin{flalign} Q_t &= \frac{dU}{dt} &\text{ [J/s]} &\\ &= C \rho V \frac{dT}{dt} &\text{ [W]} &\\ \\ \end{flalign} $$The energy balance states the total change in heat over distance dU/dx at both boundaries of a 1D element equals the change in heat over time dU/dt. Derive thermal energy rates \(\frac{dU}{dt}\), \(\frac{dU}{dx}\) from the first law of thermodynamics. Energy Balance $$ \begin{flalign} &dU = dQ \cdot dt &\text{ [J]} &\\ \\ &\text{ where: } dQ = \frac{d}{dx} \frac{dU}{dx} &\\ \\ &\frac{dU}{dt} = \frac{d}{dx} \frac{dU}{dx} &\text{ [W]} &\\ \\ &\text{where: } \frac{dU}{dt} = C \rho V \frac{dT}{dt} \text{ and } \frac{d}{dx} \frac{dU}{dx} = d\left( k A \frac{dT}{dx} \right) &\\ \end{flalign} $$ Assume the element's spatial temperature distribution is uniform, meaning the core of the element will always be the same temperature as it's surface, the mass \(\rho V \text{ [kg]}\) can be removed from eqn (2). Lumped Assumptions $$ \begin{flalign} &C \rho V \frac{dT}{dt} = d\left( k A \frac{dT}{dx} \right) &\text{ [W]} &\\ \\ &C \rho V \frac{dT}{dt} = k A \frac{dT}{dx} &\text{ No net change in } dx &\\ \\ &C \frac{dT}{dt} = k \frac{dT}{dx} &\text{ Remove mass } \rho V \text{ and } A &\\ \end{flalign} $$ Set T(t) as y(t), solve calculus $$ \begin{flalign} \frac{dT}{dt} &= \frac{k}{C} \frac{dT}{dx} &\text{ Rearranged } &\\ \\ \frac{dy}{dt} &= ky &\text{ More intuitive y form} &\\ \\ &= k e^{kt} \text{ iff } y(t) = e^{kt} &\\ \end{flalign} $$ Set y(t) back to T(t) for final form $$ \begin{flalign} \frac{dT}{dt} &= kdT &\\ &= k e^{kt} & &\\ \\ dT(t) &= e^{kt} &\text{delta T function} &\\ \end{flalign} $$ Account for dT at t=0 $$ \begin{flalign} &\text{Solve }\frac{dT}{dt} \text{ with chain rule } \mapsto \frac{d}{dx}f(g(x)) = \frac{df}{dg} \cdot \frac{dg}{dx} = \frac{df}{dx} &\\ \\ &\frac{dT}{dt} = \frac{dC}{de^{kt}} \cdot \frac{de^{kt}}{dt} = C k e^{kt} &\\ \\ &T(t) = C e^{kt} &\\ \\ &\text{where } C = T(0) \text{ and } k = k A/C &\\ \end{flalign} $$
